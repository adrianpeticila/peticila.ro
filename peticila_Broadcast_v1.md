**Subject:** You already have a brand. You didn't choose that.

---

Welcome.

Not sure why you signed up. Maybe something on the site sounded like a real person wrote it.

---

I've built a career around one uncomfortable truth:

You don't choose whether you have a brand.

You already have one.

You only choose whether you control it or let other people define it for you.

This applies to a company with 200 employees.

It applies to a freelancer who just made a LinkedIn account.

It applies to you, right now, whatever you sell and whoever you sell it to.

Your brand is what people say about you when you're not in the room. Yes, that quote is old. It survives because it's true, not because it's clever.

The problem isn't that you don't have a brand.

The problem is that most people find out what their brand is after they've already lost a client, a deal, or a job. Retroactively. With damage done.
